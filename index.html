Interpretation: The satellite mean concentration falls below the NOAA verified global background for both seasons. This reveals a fundamental characteristic of national scale passive satellite monitoring. The majority of pixels capture background air rather than emission plumes, which disperse rapidly across the 5.5 km pixel footprint. Only 14.1 percent of pixels show detectable enhancement above background. Those pixels cluster with remarkable geographic precision over known emission sources, demonstrating that satellite data functions as a geographic localization tool rather than a national inventory verification tool.

Core Finding: National scale satellite monitoring is not primarily a tool for verifying whether a country's total emission inventory is correct. It is a tool for locating where emissions originate with geographic precision that no ground based inventory system can replicate. The accountability question worth asking is whether official inventories correctly attribute emissions to the right geographic sources at the right magnitude. That is where satellite data provides genuine independent value.

Pipeline Architecture

Satellite Ingestion
ESA Sentinel 5P TROPOMI L2 methane product downloaded via Copernicus Data Space API. Parsed from netCDF4 format using xarray. Quality filtered at qa_value greater than 0.5 per ESA documentation.

Data Warehouse
Processed observations loaded to Snowflake in batches with deduplication. dbt models for staging, intermediate, and mart layers with full test coverage.

Background Selection
NOAA GML monthly means used as period specific background. January 2023: 1,919.93 ppb. Summer 2023: 1,914.89 ppb. A historical fixed value of 1,870 ppb would overestimate enhancement by approximately 50 ppb.

Limitations

Temporal Coverage
Four months covering winter and summer only. Spring and autumn data would complete the annual picture.

Pixel Footprint
TROPOMI footprint of 5.5 x 3.5 km means emission plumes are diluted by surrounding clean air, explaining why the national mean falls below background.

Orbital Sampling
Not all regions receive equal overpass frequency. Cloud cover reduces valid observations systematically.
